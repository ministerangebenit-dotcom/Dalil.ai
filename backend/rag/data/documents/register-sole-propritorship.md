# How to Register a Sole Proprietorship in Cameroon

## Overview
Registering a sole proprietorship takes 2-4 weeks and costs 45,000-80,000 FCFA.

## Step 1: Certificate of Non-Conviction
Visit the Court of First Instance. Bring: National ID, 2 passport photos (white background), 5,000 FCFA stamp. Time: 1-3 days. Cost: ~6,000 FCFA. Risk: colored backgrounds rejected.

## Step 2: RCCM Registration
Submit to the Trade Registry: Certificate of Non-Conviction, lease agreement or title deed, RCCM form. Time: 3-7 days. Cost: 25,000-50,000 FCFA. Risk: incomplete form delays.

## Step 3: Tax Identification Number
Go to the Centre des Impôts with RCCM certificate and ID. Time: 1-2 days. Free.

## Step 4: CNPS Registration
If you have employees, register with CNPS. Time: 1 week. Cost: income-based. Risk: fines if late.

## Common Mistakes
- Using colored photo background
- Residential lease without commercial mention
- Certificate of Non-Conviction expires after 3 months
